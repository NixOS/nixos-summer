This summer it is time to learn Nix.

A two month train and work project that will start in July 2021. This program is great for anyone who wants to rapidly increase their Nix skills while doing useful work.

The gist of it

The Summer of Nix is a two month train and work project that will take place from August 2nd to September 30th 2021. It is made for anyone who wants to rapidly increase their Nix skills while doing useful work for the Nix and the wider FOSS community - all of this in a remote team, at your favourite place and getting paid a nice stipend. After the program, there will be opportunities to continue longer term as paid freelance contractor with ad-hoc Nix packaging tasks.

Who is organizing this, and why?

The program is funded via the European Comission's Next Generation Internet Initiative, and organized by members of the core Nix development team, the NixOS foundation, the NLnet foundation and Tweag. The wider goal is to establish Nix as one of the principal platforms for free and open source software. Concretely, this means packaging about 250 free and open programs that are supported by the NLNet foundation, and improving the general packaging situation in Nix. This program's approach is to train a team about 20 participants to become at ease in the Nix ecosystem and to deliver packaged, ready-to-use software together.

For whom is this interesting?

Students, professionals or passionates who want to rapidly increase their Nix skills while doing useful, paid work are all welcome. Experienced Nix packagers are also very welcome to participate in a mentoring role. What we need is time commitment, motivation and basic knowledge of Nix (see below).

How can I apply?

Send an email to summer@nixos.org filling out the template below: Who are you? _6 reasonably long phrases maximum_ Why are you interested in participating in this program? _6 reasonably long phrases maximum_ What are you comfortable with and what not in the Nix world? _6 reasonably long phrases maximum_ Which other software projects did you work on? _6 reasonably long phrases maximum_ An organizer will contact you within 7 days and do a short interview.
